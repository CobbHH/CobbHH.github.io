Before the Graphical User Interface, Mouse, and Household Computer

Before the turning point computers were inaccessible, this was often caused by the unintuitive design of the machines, and the learning curve that came with them, or the unreliability or speed of the computers.

Marlyn Wescoff (left) and Ruth Lichterman (right) programming the ENIAC. (ENIAC Display)
Some of the 17,468 vacuum tubes that were used in the ENIAC computer .(ENIAC Display)

To understand why computers before the introduction of the GUI and then mouse were inaccessible, we have to go to the beginning of computers. The ENIAC was … that used vacuum tubes, these vacuum tubes were large and unreliable and would often break… The ENIAC was also programmed with punched cards, which was slow and tedious (SOURCE). The invention of the transistor at Bell Labs solved the problems of vacuum tubes as they were reliable and very small (University of Texas). Here is what a modern-day transistor looks like.

The invention of the integrated circuits / computer chips were also an important part leading up to the turning point, as it allowed computers to do processes more efficiently (University of Texas). This performance improvement contributed to the turning point by enabling the processing power needed for a bitmap display (Bruce horn).

The first integrated circuit invented by Jack Kilby (Jack Kilby's Intergrated Circuit).

The displays were made with analog circuits, and dots were used to represent other ships on the radar. (Akass, Clive)

Since computer components decreased in material cost and became more reliable, household computers became more affordable, however they were still inaccessible as they were unintuitive and had a large learning curve (Edwards, Alistair). The invention of the computer chip and memory were also an important part leading up to the turning point, as it allowed computers to do processes more efficiently. This enabled the ability to create usable bitmap displays (Bruce Horn).
